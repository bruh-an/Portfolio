SnapBudget Overview

SnapBudget is a mobile budgeting app I co-developed with a partner that helps users track expenses by taking photos or uploading images of receipts. The app uses Optical Character Recognition (OCR) to extract information such as the total cost, date, and vendor name, then organizes the expense into spending categories. Our goal was to create a simple budgeting tool for anybody who wishes to track spending without connecting their bank accounts.

SnapBudget Initial Concept

In the early planning stage, we focused on building a lightweight alternative to traditional budgeting apps. The original concept included Firebase authentication, receipt scanning, local storage, cloud storage, and spending summaries. We also sketched out the main screens, including the login page, home dashboard, camera page, history page, and settings page.

SnapBudget Development Process

After building the early prototype, we asked others to test the app for feedback on the main flow, focusing on elements such as UI/UX, scanning/uploading a receipt, and viewing saved receipts in the history page. Based on that feedback, we polished the interface by cleaning up the overall appearance, improving the camera screen layout, adjusting button placement, and making the image preview easier to understand. These changes helped make the app feel more usable and less like a rough technical demo.

SnapBudget Challenges

The biggest challenge was getting OCR to work properly. Google Cloud Vision had trouble reading iPhone receipt images because some files were saved as HEIF, so we had to convert them to JPEG first. We also used regex patterns to pull useful receipt information and added an OpenAI API fallback in case the OCR failed.

SnapBudget Final Results

By the final version, SnapBudget included Firebase Authentication, functioning OCR, Google Places categorization, AsyncStorage caching, and a review modal where users could correct receipt details before saving. Overall, this collaborative project shows my ability to build functional app prototypes, work through technical problems, and improve an idea through iteration.
